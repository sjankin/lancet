Human Rights Principles Principle 1 : Businesses should support and respect the protection of internationally proclaimed human rights; and Principle 2 : Make sure that they are not complicit in human rights abuses, assessment, policy and goals Russ Consulting Sdn Bhd highly respects universal human rights and freedom and therefore its business conduct and practice is done in a regard that adheres to United Nations Global Compact (UNGC) human rights’ principles. As a service-centric company, we believe that people should always come first. The company views each and every employee as the company’s asset and treat each individual equally. Every employee is given equal rights and opportunity during recruitment and as well as in employment. The “people always come first” concept is not only practiced within our internal environment but is also extended to other stakeholders such as our wide array of clientele, partners, suppliers and vendors. 1) Race, colour, religion and politics Over at Russ Consulting Sdn Bhd, every individual is given the opportunity to be a family member of the company regardless of race, colour, religion and political beliefs. All decisions are reached on the sole basis of employees’ performance and reputation. No bias and prejudice is practiced whether it relates to appointment, promotion, remuneration or dismissal. Russ treasures Malaysia’s culture and tradition richness and indirectly promotes 1Malaysia concept by celebrating all the main festivities with its culturally diversified employees. The company reviews its staff performance and remuneration based on merits every two years. 2) Gender The public relations industry attracts a fair number of female employees. Although the company has a higher ratio of female workers compared to male workers, balance in the workplace is sustained as both genders are equally capable and competent in their profession. 1 3) Physical disabilities The company is not prejudicial towards people with physical disabilities. Staff are hired and retained based on their performance. In fact, Russ have previously hired a staff with Down syndrome ailment. 4) Workshops and trainings Russ practices the motto “Responsibility, Response and Reach”. As a dynamic company, we believe in the need to develop our staff’s skills and knowledge. Being an ever changing industry, public relations practitioners need to be well equipped with relevant and current “it” skills and trends. Workshops and trainings provide knowledge input and exposure to employees and thus helping them to serve our clients better. 5) Annual Leave The company grants staff a total of 8 days of paid annual leave for the employment period of less than 2 years; 12 days for 2 years or more but less than 5 years and 6 days for our staff who have been employed for 5 years as stipulated by the Malaysian Labour Law. Staff are also given rights to all national and state public holidays. The company believes strongly in the Code of Conduct and will carry out investigations if they have been violated in any manner. Implementation Russ employees are introduced to the Code of Conduct upon appointment in which each staff will be given a copy of the Employee’s Handbook for their reference. They are required to have a thorough read on the document and any inquiries and doubts shall be directed to be attended by the administration executive. Principle 4 : The elimination of all forms of forced and compulsory labour; Russ Consulting does not employ or cordon any form of forced or compulsory labour. Since the public relations industry matters on people, we highly believe in “people” values and therefore take into great care maintaining good relationships with all stakeholders and this also includes our employees as well. We have treated our employees with great attention and respect and will continue to do so in the future. Employees are sent for training and programmes to better equip them with relevant knowledge and skills thus providing opportunities for career development and enhancement. However, we are mindful that our company can become associated with forced labour through our business links with contractors and suppliers. We have to be more aware of the forms and causes of forced labour, as well as how it might occur in different industries. 3 Measurement and outcomes The company will be more cautious about labour practices of our associate and make them aware of our own principles in this regard. Implementation Russ Consulting Sdn Bhd will constantly communicate to contractors and suppliers with regard to our stand against forced or compulsory labour. Principle 5 : The effective abolition of child labour; and Our company believes that children are leaders of tomorrow and therefore we avoid any form of involvement and engagement that may put children at danger and violates their rights. As stipulated by the Children and Young Persons (Employment) Act of Malaysia that prohibits the employment of children younger than the age of 14. We believe that conduciveness of the work place contributes to workers’ productivity level. Indirectly this means happy workers will ensure better work quality and this in some way will also contribute to business prosperity. 4 Thus, over here at Russ Consulting Sdn Bhd, we are strongly against the malpractice of distinction, exclusion or preference made regardless of any grounds (race, religion, gender, personal connections, etc.).
