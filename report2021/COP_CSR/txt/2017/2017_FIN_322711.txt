Since 2009 Cramo participates in the United Nations Global Compact; the largest voluntary corporate citizenship in the world. This entails a commitment to actively support the implementation of the United Nations’ fundamental principles in the areas of human rights, labour rights, environmental sustainability and anti-corruption. Within the frame of the UN Global Compact Cramo has formed its Code of Conduct for all employees as well as its Code of Conduct for suppliers and business partners. The participation is a group level decision but applies to all units in the Cramo Group. The Global Compact serves as an overall commitment and guiding principles for Cramos structured approach to social responsibility, taking the subsidiaries different starting points in terms of local conditions and experiences into consideration. Internally, the progress is monitored via the local business planning processes and in internal audits. Externally, the progress is reported to the Global Compact Office on a yearly basis and made public in the annual report and the sustainability report. “I am pleased to confirm that Cramo Group reaffirms its support of the Ten Principles of the United Nations Global Compact in the areas of Human Rights, Labour, Environment and Anti-Corruption. In the 2016 Communication on Progress (Cramo Care – sustainability report) we describe our actions to integrate the Global Compact and its principles into our business strategy, culture and daily operations. We are also committed to share this information with all our stakeholders using our primary channels of communication.” Yours sincerely, Leif Gustafsson President and CEO Cramo Plc (Cramo Group)
