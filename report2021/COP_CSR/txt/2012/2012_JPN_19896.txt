Takehiko Yamamoto President Executive Officer 02 Annual Report 2012 DAIBIRU CORPORATION FISCAL 2011 BUSINESS PERFORMANCE In fiscal 2011, although a recovery trend was seen in some parts of the Japanese economy following the initial downturn resulting from the effects of the catastrophic East Japan Earthquake that occurred in March 2011, the overall economic climate remained challenging as corporate earnings declined due to the flooding in Thailand last fall, the sharply appreciating yen, and other factors. The fiscal second half saw a move toward a yen correction and a recovery in stock prices, but the future remains uncertain amid concerns about the economic slowdown overseas. In the office leasing sector in Japan, vacancy rates still remained high and rent levels continued to be sluggish. Despite such environment, the Daibiru Group maintained aggressive sales activities and implemented new measures to further improve tenant services, including remodeling existing buildings. These efforts enabled the Company to maintain high overall occupancy rates. In April 2011, Daibiru established its first overseas representative office in Ho Chi Minh City, Vietnam, with a strong desire to develop business in Vietnam. In January 2012, we acquired Saigon Tower, an office building located in the central business district (18 floors above-ground, 2 floors below-ground and 17,986 m2 of gross floor area), through an equity purchase of the holding company (Jentower Limited). Furthermore, within Japan, we have been looking into development of businesses catering to demand generated by an aging society, and in December 2011 acquired Clara Saginuma, a nursing home property (Kawasaki City, Kanagawa Prefecture, 4 floors above-ground, 1 floor below-ground and 2,696 m2 of gross floor area). Both facilities are expected to generate stable earnings over the long term for the Company. In addition, construction of the Daibiru-Honkan Building is proceeding smoothly at the site of the former Daibiru Building, with completion scheduled at the end of February 2013. Also, construction began on the New Shin Daibiru Building (provisional name) (Dojimahama, Kita-ku, Osaka), another larger scale office property currently in the development stage. Last year, in the aftermath of the earthquake, we faced electricity power supply shortages to our buildings in the peak demand periods of summer and winter. In response, we implemented a variety of measures to reduce electricity consumption at our buildings, and, with the understanding and cooperation of our tenants and everyone else involved, we were able to meet our initial targets. As a result, we posted consolidated revenue from operations of ¥33,671 million, down ¥1,167 million, or 3.3%, year on year. Operating income declined by ¥122 million, or 1.2%, to ¥10,162 million, and ordinary income increased ¥16 million, or 0.2%, to ¥8,910 million. Income before income taxes was ¥7,885 million, a year-on-year increase of ¥1,306 million, or 19.8%. Net income climbed ¥1,032 million, or 27.8%, to ¥4,740 million. As part of our strategy for expansion of the business domain, we have been studying ways to develop an office leasing business in Vietnam as well as how to cultivate business created by the aging population in Japan. As our first actions in these areas, in fiscal 2011 we acquired the Saigon Tower and Clara Saginuma. Saigon Tower is an A-class office building with 18 above-ground floors and 2 below-ground floors, and was completed in 1996. It is located on Le Duan Blvd., in the central part of Ho Chi Minh City, a prime location surrounded by the consulate generals of the U.S., France and the UK. Many of the building’s tenants are well-known global companies, and it is close to full occupancy with an operating rate of 98%. Vietnam is a market promised for future growth, and we feel that there is considerable significance for us to acquire a high-quality property already in operation and join as one of the market players before carrying out a new development on our own. Meanwhile, given the growing elderly population in Japan, Daibiru sees the growth potentiality for facilities providing services to the elderly people in the coming decades, and has been exploring business opportunities in this sphere. As its first investment, Daibiru acquired Clara Saginuma. Located in a suburban area with a good living environment as well as in close proximity to the Tokyo metropolitan area, the property is currently leased and operated by Benesse Style Care Co., Ltd., the largest nursing home operator in Japan. Daibiru will not be directly engaged in the business of providing nursing care to seniors. Nursing care service operators are in need of reliable property owners who will own properties for a long period of time, so our aim is to acquire or develop blue-chip properties and grow our earnings base. In the office leasing industry, we expect the environment to remain challenging, as the supply-demand balance is expected to weaken due to another mass supply of office buildings amid weak demand for office space. The Daibiru Group will continue to maintain and further strengthen its business model based on developing firstclass office buildings in the prime business districts of Tokyo and Osaka, and operating them over the long term with dedicated property management resources. By so doing, we strive to achieve balanced growth over the long term.
