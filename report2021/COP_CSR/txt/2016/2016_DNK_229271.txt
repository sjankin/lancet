GB Human Rights Principle 1: Businesses should support and respect the protection of internationally proclaimed human rights; and Principle 2: Make sure that they are not complicit in human right abuses. What we want We are an international, open and accommodating workplace. Kentaur A/S supports compliance with international human rights. We do not accept discrimination on the basis of race, religion, gender, age or disability. It is important for us that the disabled, women, people of a non-Danish background or others with special needs are visible in the workplace and are given an equal opportunity to build a career. We want to increase our focus on diversity at all levels in the organisation. We work continuously to create an inclusive workplace with room for diversity. We believe that diversity creates value for the company and expands our horizon. What we do We have focused on international human rights in our diversity policy, which we are still in the process of implementing in our organization. This means that when hiring, promoting or dismissing we are careful not to violate fundamental human rights conventions. Our CSR (Corporate Social Responsibility) programme describes our initiatives related to end users, the environment and the people producing our clothes. Human rights and basic employee standards must be respected wherever we operate. Together with our partners, our CSR work will ensure that we respect human rights. We are, therefore, participants in the BSCI (Business Social Compliance Initiative). BSCI is an association of more than 1,400 European companies that share a common set of ethical rules, a Code of Conduct, in order to establish requirements to ensure decent working conditions in the supply chain. When entering into a partnership ,Kentaur requires, as a minimum, all of our existing and future suppliers to sign our Code of Conduct, thus agreeing with our and BSCI guidelines. All of our suppliers with a buying volume (BV) over 1% have signed our BSCI Code of Conduct. We have also bolstered the requirements, meaning that all future suppliers must undergo a screening by Kentaur. The screening will assess whether the supplier and the factory can meet our and BSCI’s guidelines and become BSCI approved within three years. If the factory does not become BSCI approved within four years, our partnership with the factory will cease. This has allowed Kentaur’s sourcing strategy to emphasize the sourcing of fabrics from suppliers already BSCI or SA8000 audited. Kentaur keep itself up to date with these guidelines, and trades with existing suppliers in accordance with BSCI audits and reporting. Meaning that if audits are not passed, we will push the supplier to undergo a re-audit. What we have achieved Our common values and our focus on the diversity policy has resulted in the number of employees with nationalities other than Danish increasing over the last years. Our objective for 20142015 was, therefore, 22%. Unfortunately, we must report that this upward trend has not continued into 2015, and our current level is 14%. The reason for the declining number is that we over the past year have hired 6 additional people, while two of our non-Danish employees have chosen to seek new challenges. We will consciously work to ensure that this figure increases again in line with our recruitment. In 2014-2015 Kentaur chose to move part of its production from a BSCI audited production site to the newly opened factory in Vietnam, which has not been BSCI audited at the time of writing. Which is why 64% of our production sites are currently BSCI audited, as opposed to 71% last year. The new factory in Vietnam has undergone a pre-audit and is ready for its first audit in April 2016. So the figure is expected to rise again next year. There will be more on this in the next COP report. Vi arbejder med stadig større fokus på genbrug af vores pap og erhvervsaffald og undersøger pt., hvilke muligheder vi har for at optimere på dette, bl.a. om vi skal invistere i en ballepresser. Mere om dette i næste COP rapport. Hvad har vi opnået I 2014-2015 er erhvervsaffald desværre steget fra 145 kg pr. medarbejder til 161 kg. Pap til genanvendelse er steget fra 47 kg pr. medarbejder til 69 kg. Stigningen kan forklares med, at vi sidste år var i gang med en større oprydningsproces. En proces, som er med til at fremme arbejdsmiljøet. I forhold til brugen af elektricitet var vores mål for 2014-2015 en reducering fra 2399 KwH til 2300KwH. Vi realiserer 2163 KwH, hvilket ligger en væsentlig del under målsætning i 2016 på 2300 KwH. Med udskiftning af lyset på lageret til LED arbejder vi fremadrettet på en yderligere reducering af brugen af KwH. Desuden har vi 2014-2015 opnået en reducering af brugen af MwH, så vi allerede sidste år ligger på 184,3 MwH, 0,7 under målsætning for 2016. I 2014-2015 er 99% af vores metervarer og tilbehør Oeko Tex Standard 100 certificeret fra leverandørerne. I 2014-2015 er vores egenproducerede produkter certificeret med EU-blomsten på 39,6% i modsætning til sidste år, hvor den lå på 47%. Nedgangen i procenten skyldes, at man fra Kentaur har valgt at beholde den samme producent på den hvide metervare men flytte den fra Europa til Asien, hvorfor der ikke længere er EU-Blomst på. Grunden til, at man fra Kentaurs side fastholder producenten og metervaren, på trods af, at den flyttes, skyldes dels vores kunders store krav til en ensartet kvalitet, hvorfor det vil være for risikabelt at skifte kvalitet. Endvidere minimeres fragten af metervaren, idet den nu fragtes fra indonesion til Vietnam frem for fra Italien til Vietnam, hvilket vil afspejle sig i vores fragtregnskab. Desuden informeres vi om foråret 2015, at selvsamme metervareleverandør muligvis ikke vil forlænge EU-blomst certifikatet. Årsagen til dette er, at kravene til indholdet af økologisk bomuld er blevet så store, at det ikke længere vil være muligt for producenten at håndhæve. Derfor afventer vi metervareleverandøren, hvorfor det ligeledes vil være umuligt at sætte nye mål for EU-Blomst certificering. Mere om dette emne i næste COP rapport. Vi har igennem 2014-2015 researchet i forhold til nye og langt mere bæredygtige metervarer. I 2013-2014 tilføjede vi TENCEL® til vores produktsortiment, og efterspørgslen på denne metervarer er stadig stigende i 2014-2015. I forbindelse med fordelingen af transport på henholdsvis fly, truck og sø, har back-office også i 2014-2015 haft flytransport som et væsentligt fokusområde. Det har resulteret i en yderligere reducering af flyfragt fra 6% i 2013-2014 til i dag 4%. En procent under vores oprindelige målsætning for 2014-2015. Fragt med skib er status quo på 57%, og fragt med lastbil er steget fra 37% til 39%. Vi har fortsat fokus på flyfragt, så vi i 2015-2016 når vores målsætning på 3%. Vi har gennemført endnu en containerkontrol i 2014-2015, og alle værdier lå under de foruddefinerede grænseværdier fra Arbejdstilsynet (DK), således at containeren og dens indhold ikke indeholdte skadelige kemikalier og/eller gasarter. GB Environment Principle 7: Businesses should support a precautionary approach to environmental challenges ; Principle 8: Undertake initiatives to promote greater environmental responsibility; and Principle 9: encourage the development and diffusion of environmentally friendly technologies What we want At Kentaur, responsibility for the environment is a core value. We want to operate our company in as environmentally friendly a way as possible. Not just because looking after the environment is profitable, but because we believe the future is green. We want to reduce the impact of our business on the environment, and we want to be a credible environmentally responsible partner to our customers. We want to support green technologies and contribute to increasing knowledge of environmentally friendly products and fabric choices. What we do We integrate environmental considerations into our business on market terms, i.e. we want to run a profitable business that respects the environment as well as the people in it. In our environmental policy, we define the areas in which we wish to prioritise our resources in terms of cooperation partners, locally and globally. We want to focus on the environmental impact of producing clothes and especially of the transport of the clothes to Denmark. We track data on the share of transport by air, sea and land. In 2014-15, we continued to work on migrating the transport options we use away from air to sea and land. Our back office therefore focused increasingly on our use of air freight in 2014-15. We are innovative and think in new ways and new materials when we choose fabrics. We mainly use fabrics and accessories which are certified to Oeko Tex Standard 100 from the supplier. In 2015, we want to ensure that our finished products are also certified to Oeko Tex Standard 100, and Kentaur will strive to attain its own Oeko Tex certificate. This will mean that the Danish Technological Institute will regularly inspect our use of chemicals and all fabric, accessory and finished product certificates. This continuous recording and validation of our products will form a significant part of our chemicals programme going forward. We will continue to focus on reducing the use of harmful chemicals in the manufacture of our clothes. Our aim is to continue to increase our percentage of environmentally certified fabrics and accessories certified to such standards as the EU Ecolabel. We will regularly inspect our containers for chemicals. We record our use of resources in-house so that we are able to reduce our waste as well as heating and electricity consumption and set measurable KPIs. What we have achieved In 2014-2015, commercial waste has unfortunately increased from 145 kg per employee to 161 kg. Cardboard for recycling has risen from 47 kg per employee to 69 kg. The increase is explained by the fact that we undertook a major clean-up process last year. A process that is helping to improve the work environment. As regards the use of electricity, our goal for 2014-2015 was a reduction from 2399 KwH to 2300KwH. We achieved 2163 KwH, which is significantly below the target in 2016 of 2300 KwH. With the replacement of the lights in the warehouse with LED we work prospectively on further reduction of the use of KwH. Furthermore, in 2014-2015 we managed to minimise the use of MwH. Last year we already achieved 184.3 MwH, 0.7 below the target for 2016. In 2014-2015, 99% of the fabrics and accessories from our suppliers were Oeko Tex Standard 100 certified. In 2014-2015, our internally manufactured products were certified with the EU flower of 39.6%, as opposed to last year, when it was 47%. The decline in the ratio is due to Kentaur having chosen to keep the same manufacturer for the white fabric but to move it from Europe to Asia, which is why there is no longer an EU Flower on it. The reason Kentaur retained the manufacturer and fabric, despite the fact that it has moved, is partly due to our customers’ demands for consistent quality and because it would be too risky to change the quality. Furthermore, the transportation of the fabric has been minimized as it is now shipped from Indonesia to Vietnam instead of from Italy to Vietnam, which will be reflected in our freight accounts. Furhermore, we were informed in the spring of 2015 that the same fabric supplier may not extend the EU Flower certificate. The reason for this is that the organic cotton content has become so large that it will no longer be possible for the manufacturer to enforce. Which is why we are awaiting the fabric supplier, which is also why it would be impossible to set new goals for the EU Flower certification. There will be more on this topic in the next COP report. Throughout 2014-2015, we researched new and far more sustainable fabrics. In 2013-2014, we added TENCEL® to our product range, and the demand for this fabric is still increasing in 2014-2015. In connection with the distribution of transport between plane, truck and sea in 2014-2015, the back-office has also focussed significantly on air freight. This has resulted in a further reduction in air freight, from 6% in 2013-2014 to the current 4%. One percent below our original target for 2014-2015. Sea transport remains the same at 57%, and freight by truck has increased from 37% to 39%. We continue to focus on air freight, in order to achieve our target of 3% in 2015-2016. We have undertook another container control in 2014-2015, and all values were below the predefined limits of the Danish Working Environment Authority (DK), meaning that the container and its contents did not contain harmful chemicals and/or gases. GB Anti-Corruption Principle 10: Businesses should work against cooruption in all its forms, including extortion and bribery What we want Being open and honest in the way we do business is a core Kentaur value. That is why we do not accept bribery, corruption or any other form of illegal incentives. We will work against corruption and bribery of any kind in our attempts to run a healthy and profitable business. We attempt to influence our partners to avoid using corruption with the purpose of remaining objective. What we do We work on our anti-corruption policy which is to ensure that Kentaur operates in an open and honest fashion. We do not offer, promise or demand bribes or other unlawful advantages to achieve or retain our business opportunities. This is regardless of whether this is at employee level or in the company as a whole. We do not pay any form of protection money to achieve a competitive advantage, and no employee is permitted to give or receive inappropriate work-related gifts. We do not financially support political parties or organisations with political objectives that do not respect basic human rights. We have decided that all employees must be given the opportunity to put forward complaints, suspicions or concerns anonymously to the company through our whistleblower scheme. We will ensure that 100% of cases are processed. What we have achieved We have formulated a detailed anti-corruption policy, which focuses on corruption and bribery, regardless of the fact that this subject is very distant from the normal work practices of a typical Scandinavian company.
