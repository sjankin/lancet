SAFETY Indus Motor Company aims to demonstrate responsible corporate conduct throughout the entire spectrum of its activities and operations. From providing high quality and safe products through promoting an integrated three-part initiative: “People, Vehicle and Traffic Environment, we ensure that all our employees are provided with a safe working environment. Safe Working Condition: • A safe work place is the prime objective of our organization. We strive to inculcate safety mindset in our workforce through comprehensive safety training and skill enhancement Communication on Progress| 12/8/2015 programs for our own and contractor employees. During the year 2014-15, we engaged an external consultant to undertake Fire Risk Assessment and further strengthened our fire prevention and detection system. • Our safety theme “Let’s make IMC Zero Accident Workplace” commenced with company’s top management signing off on their commitment to safety, Fire-fighting training and Fire Simulation Evacuation Drills, a 5S competition and awareness sessions on defensive driving, first-aid, etc. to further strengthen the effectiveness of our systems and to counter emergency situations. • In order to identify and eliminate hazards in the workplace, a competition “4-Rounds of Keiken Yuichi” amongst all employees was arranged. The competition drew an enthusiastic response with winners recognized, rewarded and their proposals incorporated as safety kaizen in the workplace. 1 INDUS MOTOR COMPANY LTD • At Indus Motor, all our employees are provided the Personal Protective Equipment (PPE) as per their job requirement. • Being a Toyota affiliate, we endeavor to follow Toyota Motor Corporation (TMC) Global Safety Standards and are subject to their audit. • We implement the Japanese 5S concept which stands for Sifting, Sorting, Sweeping, Spick/Span and Supreme Attitude. These five elements mainly focus on improving cleanliness and working condition. Top Management conducts 5S-Walks of the plant and work places and audits against standard at regular intervals. • We strictly follow the safety guidelines as per Plant Management Requirement (PMR) under KI-J program of our principal, Toyota Motor Corporation, Japan (TMC).
