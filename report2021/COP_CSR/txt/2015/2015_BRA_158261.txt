APPENDICES SOCIAL INVESTMENTS KEY SOCIAL INITIATIVES SUPPORTED IN 2014 Project/social program Goal Furnas Social Improve the quality of life and the social development of communities, contributing to the full exercise of Programs citizenship. In 2014, by providing support for 244 institutions in 146 municipalities, some 270,000 people were benefited. The initiatives supported by the company totaled R$ 5 million for the acquisition of materials and equipment and improved facilities. One of the beneficiary institutions was the Family Farmers Association of the Our Lady of the Rosary of Três Pontas-MG, which used the funds to buy machinery for a candy factory. Social partnership The company supports social projects that generate employment and income, health, food projects security, education, sports, environment and promotion of citizenship. In 2014, 13 projects were supported, with an investment of approximately R$ 1.1 million, benefiting 1,356 people. Projects for Encouraged territorial development through the collective construction of change processes, strengthening Community local communities. In 2014, 10 reference projects were monitored in the field of jobs and income Integration generation, sports and education. In total, over 29,000 people have benefited from the various actions. United Nations The company has worked to achieve the Millennium Development Goals, by supporting decentralization as Development seminars to contribute to a post-2015 development agenda. Through a partnership with UNDP the company Program (UNDP) has been working for the construction of a sustainable future, giving priority to combating poverty and inequality, strengthening democratic governance, economic growth and sustainable development. Furnas supported the publication of the Brazil Atlas, for the Human Development Report (HDR) 2014. Community Farming The company has contributed to the fight against hunger and poverty since 2003 through its food security and Seedling program. Currently, Furnas maintains seven community gardens and plant nurseries on its premises. Fruits and Nursery Project vegetables are distributed weekly. In 2014, 57 tons of foodstuffs were distributed in São José da Barra (MG) alone, benefiting 77 families and students in public schools, serving a thousand meals daily. In Foz do Iguaçu (PR), 104,000 seedlings were distributed to more than 7,000 people in 23 institutions, such as schools and prisons. Vila Santa Tereza For 17 years the company has supported the community center in the municipality of Belford Community Center Roxo (RJ), the only such facility in the area. Its premises feature a multi-purpose court, which the community can use for sports activities, and rooms for training workshops. The company has implemented a program to eradicate child labor. In 2014, it promoted courses in fabric cutting and sewing, handicrafts and hairdressing. A total of seven classes trained 101 people. Volunteer Activities Introduced in 2002, the program supports the voluntary actions of its employees. It encourages Program employees to develop projects to improve living conditions in nearby communities. In 2014, it introduced the IX Social Projects and Actions Contest, which received submissions from 121 projects. Brazil Kitchen Project Conducted in partnership with SESI, this project is designed to educate the population in poor communities about the handling and preparation of low-cost, high nutrition meals. In 2014, 24 “Brazilian cooking classes” were taught, promoted in different neighborhoods in Brasília (DF) and municipalities in Espírito Santo, which together were attended by more than 2,886 persons. Combating child A signatory of the Declaration of Commitment to Confront the Sexual Exploitation of Children and exploitation Teenagers, in 2014 the Company signed a Cooperation Agreement to draw up a protocol to develop actions for construction projects. In 2014, various campaigns and activities were prepared in partnership with the Municipal Social Development Secretariat during Carnival in Rio de Janeiro. The company also participated in actions to combat exploitation and sexual abuse of children and teenagers on Brazilian highways, promoted by the Mão Certa (Right Track) Program, created in 2006 by Childhood, which is a partner.
