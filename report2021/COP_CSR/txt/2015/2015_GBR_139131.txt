8th April 2015 To our stakeholders: I am pleased to confirm that BayWa r.e. Solar Systems Ltd reaffirms its support of the Ten Principles of the United Nations Global Compact in the areas of Human Rights, Labour, Environment and Anti-Corruption. In this annual Communication on Progress, we describe our actions to continually improve the integration of the Global Compact and its principles into our business strategy, culture and daily operations. We also commit to share this information with our stakeholders using our primary channels of communication. Sincerely yours, Ian Draisey Managing Director Human Rights: Assessment, policy BayWa r.e. Solar Systems Ltd actively supports the Universal Declaration of Human Rights. We will not carry out business with any countries or regimes where flagrant human rights abuses are known, e.g torture, politically motivated disappearances, etc. BayWa r.e. Solar Systems Ltd has a Dignity at Work policy and is committed to ensuring that all our employees are treated fairly and with respect. In the coming year we will work with our significant supply chain partners to see if they are signatories to the Global Compact and if they are not to actively encourage them to support and join the Compact. Implementation BayWa r.e. Solar Systems Ltd has a Grievance Procedure in place and offers mediation to staff at work as a first stage resolution to any problems experienced in the work place. Where there are changes within the organisation, extensive consultation is undertaken to ensure staff are involved in the process. We have a range of Human Resources Policies which reflect BayWa r.e’s proactive stance on human rights including our Dignity at Work Policy, Equal Opportunities Policy and Flexible Working Policy. Measurement of outcomes In the past year BayWa r.e has not been subject to any investigations, legal cases or incidents involving Human Rights violations. Labour: Assessment, policy 1 BayWa r.e. Solar Systems Ltd supports the ILO Core Conventions and will not do business with any organisation who uses forced or child labour. All BayWa r.e. Solar Systems Ltd employees are issued with a contract of employment which clearly states their terms and conditions including pay rates and ‘time in lieu’ arrangements. All staff have access to a Company Handbook which includes information on standard terms and conditions of employment, company benefits, company rules, grievance and disciplinary procedures. Implementation BayWa r.e. Solar Systems Ltd consults with staff on a continuous basis – each quarter we hold a Company Meeting which everyone attends and where the MD does a re-cap on the past quarter, appraising staff of what has been achieved, and where we are going. BayWa r.e. Solar Systems Ltd has a range of HR Policies which support best practice in terms of labour and employment including Equality & Diversity Policy, and Flexible Working Policy. Measurement of outcomes At the most senior level there are 3 female managers and 3 male managers with a range of ages. BayWa r.e. Solar Systems Ltd works in areas which are by their nature quite male dominated e.g electrical installations, but this in no way reflects our approach to encouraging and promoting staff within the organisation. We hold appraisals quarterly to identify personal development needs and training requirements and these are actioned where-ever financially possible. BayWa r.e. Solar Systems Ltd has not been involved in any investigations, legal cases or other relevant events related to the contravention of the Global Compact Labour principles. Environment: Assessment, policy BayWa r.e. Solar Systems Ltd have an Environmental Policy. The BayWa AG group (which BayWa r.e . Solar Systems Ltd is a part of) recently published a Sustainability Brochure. In the coming year BayWa r.e. Solar Systems Ltd’s main environmental target is to reduce our landfill waste and increase our percentage of recyclable waste. Implementation 1 Adopted in 1998, the ILO Declaration on Fundamental Principles and Rights at Work is an expression of commitment by governments, employers' and workers' organizations to uphold basic human values values that are vital to our social and economic lives BayWa r.e. Solar Systems Ltd have provided training in waste separation and recycling to our staff and provide suitable disposal bins. BayWa r.e. Solar Systems Ltd are working with PV Cycle to reclaim all damaged and end of life Solar PV modules from the market place to be recycled, as part of the WEEE regulations. At our offices, we have motion sensitive lighting throughout our offices to reduce energy costs. Sharon Speake our Customer Services/Compliance Manager is responsible for our Health & Safety and for environmental protection within BayWa r.e. Solar Systems Ltd. Measurement of outcomes BayWa r.e. Solar Systems Ltd has not had any environmental incidents within the last year and has not been subject to any statutory notices or prosecutions. Anti-Corruption: Assessment, policy BayWa r.e. Solar Systems Ltd senior management have received a briefing on the Bribery Act and we will not participate in any corruption, bribery or extortion. BayWa r.e. Solar Systems Ltd supports the UN Convention Against Corruption and we will not operate in countries or with organisations who are corrupt. Implementation BayWa r.e. Solar Systems Ltd have an Anti-Bribery Policy and an Anti-Trust Policy in place which is committed to zero tolerance policy towards bribery and corruption and is committed to acting fairly and with integrity in all of its business dealings and relationships wherever it operates and implementing and enforcing effective systems to counter bribery. The Financial Controller is ultimately responsible for anti-corruption within BayWa r.e. Solar Systems Ltd. Measurement of outcomes BayWa r.e. Solar Systems Ltd has not been involved in any legal cases, rulings or other events related to corruption and bribery. BayWa r.e. Solar Systems Ltd submits audited accounts each year to Companies House which are verified by external auditors and this is used as one of the methods of identifying any spurious payments which could be related to bribery or corrupt behaviour.
